Inflammation

Why endo lesions hurt, and what calms them down.

  • NF-κB pathwayMaster switch for chronic pain signalling. Curcumin, sulfasalazine candidates.
  • COX-2 / prostaglandinsWhy NSAIDs work. Celecoxib being explored for endo-specific dosing.
  • TNF-αDrives lesion growth. Anti-TNFs (adalimumab, infliximab) under investigation.
  • IL-6 axisElevated in peritoneal fluid. Tocilizumab being trialled in related conditions.
🌸

Excess oestrogen

Lesions are oestrogen-fuelled. Where can we starve them?

  • Aromatase activityLesions make their own oestrogen. Letrozole and anastrozole are the strongest candidates.
  • 17β-HSD type 1Local oestrogen conversion. Small-molecule inhibitors in early-stage development.
  • ERα signallingSERMs (raloxifene, bazedoxifene) explored as add-on therapy.
  • Sulfatase pathwayBackdoor oestrogen activation. Sulfatase inhibitors under preclinical study.
🛡

Immune confusion

Why the body doesn't clear ectopic tissue.

  • NK cell dysregulationNatural-killer cells fail to remove misplaced tissue. Target for immune modulation.
  • Macrophage polarisationM2 ("repair") macrophages dominate lesion sites and feed growth.
  • Treg deficiencyRegulatory T-cells under-represented. Possible biomarker for severity.
  • Autoimmune overlapHashimoto's, lupus, RA all show stronger co-occurrence. Shared pathways possible.
⚡

Nerve sensitisation

Why pain persists even after lesions are removed.

  • Nerve Growth Factor (NGF)Lesions grow new pain fibres into surrounding tissue. Anti-NGF antibodies in trial.
  • TRPV1 channelHeat + capsaicin receptor. Persistently active in chronic endo pain.
  • Central sensitisationSpinal-cord rewiring amplifies signals. Why low-dose amitriptyline can help.
  • Vagus modulationVagal tone correlates with pain levels. Stimulation studies underway elsewhere.
